03Diagnosis · Problem Validation
Funnel Diagnosis · Root Cause

It already exists. So why doesn't it stick?

Swiggy and Zomato already shipped group ordering, and it took ~6% of orders on launch weekend. So before designing anything, the honest question every PM should ask: is low usage a discovery problem we can market our way out of, or a product problem we have to build our way out of? They have opposite fixes. Here is the funnel diagnosis.

Where the funnel actually leaks stage diagnosis
Awareness
Partial
~80% of app features go rarely or never used. Group ordering is one most people have never noticed.
→
Discovery
Structural leak
Buried in the cart, and a minority-of-sessions feature, so it can't earn a permanent home-screen slot. Hard to find by design.
→
Activation
Healthy
Works when surfaced: ~6% of orders on launch weekend. Demand is genuinely there.
→
Retention
The root leak
One painful try, host pays all, no live presence, and the group reverts to the WhatsApp thread.
→
Habit
Never forms
The default stays the group chat, so usage flatlines after the launch spike.
Diagnosis: demand is proven (Activation works), so this is not a demand problem. The two leaks sit at Discovery (structural, not a marketing miss) and Retention (the real root). You cannot market your way past a retention leak.
Three hypotheses, one survives root-cause analysis
Is it a demand problem?
✕ Rejected
No. ~6% of orders on launch weekend proves people want it the moment it is put in front of them.
A discovery / marketing problem?
~ Partly
Real, but secondary and structural: the feature is buried and only relevant in a minority of sessions. Ad spend buys a spike, not a habit.
A product problem?
✓ Confirmed
Yes, the root. Coordination cost survives the current flow, so triers revert to WhatsApp. Fixing it needs presence and split-pay, not a banner.
The root cause channel mismatch
The intent lives in WhatsApp, not in the app
The moment "let's order together" happens in the group chat, before anyone opens Zomato. WhatsApp reaches roughly 550 million Indians, about 68% of the online population, at ~38 minutes a day. So an in-app group feature is in the wrong place at the moment of need: low discovery is not a marketing miss, it is a channel mismatch. The coordination starts, and dies, in a thread Zomato never sees.

Both leaks. One fix.

This is why the v1 isn't a feature, it's an entry point. The WhatsApp deep-link moves the start of the order into the channel where intent already lives (fixing Discovery virally, at zero CAC) and pulls the chaos into a live, structured cart (fixing Retention). Marketing comes after the leak is plugged, never before, and chat-to-cart already converts at 40-60% in India. Discovery and product were never two problems: they share one root, and one solution.

04Solution Design · The Live Cart
User Journey · Conceptual Wireframes

From WhatsApp + Zomato
to a live collaborative cart.

The proposed flow collapses two apps and a dozen messages into one presence-rich session. Here's the old way against the new way, then the screens that make it real.

✕ The Old Way

WhatsApp + Zomato, juggled

  1. Someone asks "should we order something?" in the group
  2. 20 messages debating the restaurant, no resolution
  3. Host opens Zomato, passes the phone around the room
  4. Items get mixed up; nobody's sure whose is whose
  5. Host pays everything, screenshots the bill to the group
  6. Chases ₹80s on UPI for three days. Some never pay.
⏱ High coordination cost · chase-down burden on one person
✓ The Proposed Way

One link, live, conflict-free

  1. Host shares a WhatsApp link / QR / room code
  2. Quick in-app vote picks the restaurant, seeds the cart
  3. Everyone adds to their own color-coded sub-cart, live
  4. Presence avatars show who's browsing / ready / paid
  5. Ready-check + host timer fires the order automatically
  6. UPI-collect splits each share, no chasing, no disputes
⚡ System-driven nudges · fairness built in · zero phone-passing

The four coordination mechanics, the crux

👥

Live presence avatars

Ambient status: browsing / added / ready / paid. Calm, not anxiety-inducing.

← Figma · Spotify Jam
🎨

Color-coded sub-carts

Per-person carts that merge, never a shared editable list. Eliminates edit conflicts with no locking.

← solves Swiggy's mix-up
✅

Ready-check lobby

Each member taps "I'm done"; host sees a live "3 of 5 ready" tally.

← gaming lobbies
⏱️

Host-overridable timer

At expiry, auto-fire with confirmed items, drop unconfirmed, extend once. Converts nagging into a system nudge.

← the biggest chase-down cut

Edge cases, decided

6 handled
📦Item sells out
Real-time flag on that item, tagged to the member. Blocks ready-check until they re-pick.
💤Member goes inactive
Presence dims to "away". Host can poke or remove; ready-check shows them not-ready.
🏃Host abandons
Transfer-host to the longest-active member, so the session never dies (the Jam weakness we don't copy).
🔒Late joiner after checkout
Cart locks on checkout. Late joiner sees "ordering has started" and can start a fresh group order.
⏱️Timer expires
Auto-fire with confirmed items; unconfirmed members dropped with a notification. Host can extend once.
₹Member never pays
In split mode, host is prompted to nudge or drop their items, never silently charged to the host.

✓ Scope In

The MVP · what ships first
  • Single restaurant, single address, one cart → one order → one rider is the solved fulfillment path
  • Host-pay default, matches current Swiggy/Zomato, optimized for speed
  • Real-time collaborative cart, presence, sub-carts, ready-check, timer, nudge
  • No-install web cart for invitees, the viral acquisition surface
  • WhatsApp-perfect deep-links + QR + room code

→ Scope Out

Deliberate trade-offs · deferred
  • Multi-restaurant, compounds prep-time, packaging & rider logic. Fast-follow.
  • Multi-address, multiplies routing, fee allocation & ETA complexity for marginal v1 value
  • Split-pay, high-value but Stage 2; host-pay clears the bar for launch
  • Labeled sub-bags, moderate effort, needs restaurant compliance; pilot later
  • Gold-gating, never. It would kill the acquisition loop. Gold becomes a demand-gen funnel instead.

08Go-To-Market · Phased Roadmap
Product Delivery · GTM

A coordination-first phased rollout.

Ship the differentiator first, monetize fairness second, delight on fulfillment third. Each stage has a benchmark that gates the next, and thresholds that would change the plan.

Stage 1 · 0–3 months · MVP

Coordination-first v1

Real-time collaborative cart: presence avatars, color-coded merge sub-carts (no locking), live totals, activity feed, ready-check, host-overridable timer with auto-fire, nudge/poke, host controls. Single restaurant, single address, host-pay default. No-install web cart for invitees. This is the differentiator, it must come first.

📊 Advance when: group orders reach ≥3–5% of food-delivery orders & ≥60% of initiated carts fire.
Stage 2 · 3–6 months · Settlement

Settlement & consensus

UPI-collect split-pay (per-person itemized + proportional fees) sends a collect request to each member's UPI app, solving the "I'll pay you back" decay Zomato promised but never shipped. Add the pre-cart restaurant vote and per-person sectioned invoices.

📊 Advance when: split-pay attach rate >25% of group orders & a measurable drop in nudges-per-order.
Stage 3 · 6–12 months · Delight

Fulfillment delight & expansion

Pilot per-person labeled sub-bags inside one master bag (proven by ezCater) with high-volume/chain partners. Large-order rider/vehicle logic. Then evaluate multi-restaurant and multi-address as fast-follows leveraging existing infrastructure.

📊 Advance when: group-order on-time rate within 2pp of solo & sub-bag NPS lift in the hostel/PG cohort.
Thresholds that change the plan: If timer-fire share stays high (>50%) and time-to-fire doesn't fall, the coordination UX isn't working, invest in nudges/defaults before scaling. If K-factor >0.3, prioritize the web-cart acquisition funnel & Gold demand-gen over fulfillment polish. If split-pay attach is low despite demand, the UPI-collect flow has friction, simplify before expanding.
09Why Zomato Wins
Business Impact · The Prize

Three prizes, one feature.

Coordination-first group ordering isn't a feature ship, it's a strategic wedge. Three compounding prizes, plus the one thing competitors can't copy.

01📈

Higher AOV

₹453 → ~₹1.7k

Group carts are multiples of solo. Swiggy logged 3.7× its usual order value on launch weekend; against Zomato's ₹453 FY25 baseline, every fired group order is worth three-to-four solo orders of contribution margin.

The basket lever
02🔁

Higher frequency

latent → booked

Coordination cost silently suppresses group occasions, the 1 AM hostel order that never happens because nobody wants to herd everyone on WhatsApp. Kill the friction and those latent moments convert into real, repeatable orders.

The occasion lever
03🚀

Viral acquisition

K-factor > 0.3

Every shared link is a CAC-free invite that drops a no-install web cart into a non-user's hands. In dense hostel / PG / office graphs the loop compounds, and Zomato Gold rides along as a demand-gen funnel, never a gate.

The growth lever
🔒 The moat

Social-graph lock-in

AOV, frequency and acquisition are wins a competitor could eventually copy. The real defensibility is the saved group: "order again with the same crew" quietly encodes each user's real-world social graph into Zomato. That's a switching cost a bare shared cart never builds, and exactly why coordination-first group ordering is a moat, not a feature.

"Both Swiggy and Zomato shipped the cart. Neither shipped the coordination. That gap, presence, ready-check, fair split, is the leapfrog."
